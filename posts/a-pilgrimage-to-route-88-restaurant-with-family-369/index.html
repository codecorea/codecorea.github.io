A pilgrimage to Route 88 restaurant with family

Namyangju-si, Gyeonggi-do, Korea

God...

Starting from the southern end of the Paldang Bridge, the 45th National Road Drive Road, winding along the riverside to the left, is dazzling with fresh green. Light sunshine knocks on the car like a piano playing from the audio. When the riverside road ends, take the road on the left side of the Toma 3-way intersection, and National Highway 88 begins. It is a road leading to Toechon-myeon. The landscape of the river that has been resumed is the Gyeongan Lake, which is connected to the Paldang Lake. Crossing the bridge and entering the Toechon-myeon area, there is the first restaurant on Route 88.

Route 88 leads to the left from the intersection leading to the Tianjin Am Holy Land. This is the starting point of the ridge of Yangpyeong, where the small shopkeepers went to go to Gwangju's small market. This place, where the cows who had struggled over their heads were fed grass and the small intestines were resting their sore legs, was called 'Sommoegi'.
Born and raised in Toechon, Mr. Jeong Ji-soo produced a Korean restaurant that served as a side dish of Cheonggukjang and herbs cooked directly on the soil of Sommegigol inherited from his father. The name is ‘Sommegigi’ (031-767-9852).
