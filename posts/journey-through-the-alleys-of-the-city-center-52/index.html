Journey through the alleys of the city center

Seocho-gu, Seoul, Korea

I think of traveling far away. However, it is urban travel that is becoming a new trend of travel for modern people who can hardly find a trip. Among them, a trip to an alleyway centered on a particular street brings new pleasures. Bangbae-dong in Seoul reminds us of a cafe alley, but there is a new way. Let's take a trip to fall with the 'Bangbae-dong Sai-gil', which is frequented up and down recently.

Bangbae-ro 42-gil, Seocho-gu, named 'Bangbaesai-gil'

The name 'bangbaesai-gil' is unique. The word "sai", meaning liver, means two or more relationships. It will be time and space to define between. In that sense, calling 42-gil as 'sai-gil' by Bangbae means not only spatial (geographical) relationship but also cultural and social relations among the people living there. Of course, '42' can be read as '시', so you can call it that way.
Saigil began to be known to the world when ten people who nested in this place tried to make the streets of culture and art. In less than two years, it has grown to a group of more than 30 people, and members participate in the form of talent donation. A representative example is the Saigil Art Map. It is the work of Kim Hee-jeong, a designer of a specialty packaging company called 'Aurum'. 'Saigil Map', drawn by hand, embodies the ideal of this street.

Quiet and secluded Bangbae-dong, what is it like?
Recently, many roads between Bangbae-dong have been reported through the media. On the holiday of the New Year's Day, I ate a late morning and went to Sai Gil. You can see signs such as 'Bangbae-dong 42-gil' and '1 ← 72' on the wall.
Different people have different tastes. Some people like crowded places, while others like quiet and secluded places. Garosu-gil was initially a secluded street, but now it's a good place for people who are turning into a complex street and want to look away. If you express Garosu-gil as a 'prose', you can say that Sai-gil is a poem. It is so quiet and secluded that it provides a space of thought.

Restaurants like galleries add to the fun of eating
Walking between two or three hours will leave your boat. I'm worried about what to eat. “Kelly Gallery & Restaurant” is eye-catching. As with most shops, the interior view through the window is a fascinating attraction. The interior is more of a gallery than an ordinary restaurant. The walls are filled with paintings, and both the table decoration and the layout are as pretty as the paintings. Ordered today's menu, duck kimchi fried rice and oil pasta. Each grain of fried rice is seasoned evenly with spicy flavors. Pasta feels alive and chewy. The combination of rich olive oil flavor, shrimp and garlic is also excellent.

Nearby is Lari Café, a popular cake shop since 1992. The shops, like a living room in a fine English home, are comfortable and antique. Made with the finest ingredients, Lari cake melts like a snowflake the moment it is put in your mouth. I ordered a chiffon cake with half the sugar content.
