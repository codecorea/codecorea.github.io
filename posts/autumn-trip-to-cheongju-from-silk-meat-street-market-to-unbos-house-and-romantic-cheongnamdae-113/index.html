Find hidden pictures in a fun way ~, Suamgol Mural Village
Suamgol, which is not far from the meat market, has the nickname 'Cheongju's last moon.' Several houses barely stood on the mountain slopes, and even the refugees settled here after the Korean War. The village, which was almost a gimmick, faced a completely different fate since 2008, when various murals were created as part of a public art project.
Suamgol Mural Village Scenery
The stories in the murals are the lives of the elderly, as the artists completed their works by directly communicating with the local people. This is why pine trees and cranes that pray for disease-free life appear, as well as laundry scenes and briquette rear cars.
Briquettes buried in the ground
The unusual thing is that the mural doesn't just stay on the wall. The briquettes that played their part completely face each other. They have speech bubbles such as 'I love you when they are broken and broken!', 'I love you just as you are' and 'Okay, spring is coming soon'.

Unbo Kim Ki-chang
In Hungdong-ri, Naesu-eup, Cheongwon-gu, Cheongju, there is Unbo Kim's house, the sister and artist of Kim Ki-chang. In 1976, after his wife and painter Park Rae-hyang passed away, Kim Ki-chang built his home in his mother's hometown and spent the rest of his life working there. Due to the rainy weather, the clouds and misty landscapes of the mountains were quite strangely matched with his hounbo. Cheongnam University Chrysanthemum Festival
I can't come back to Cheongju without seeing Cheongnamdae. It is already famous for the presidential villas, but at the time of the chrysanthemum festival, you will be able to enjoy the visually near perfection. This year's chrysanthemum festival begins on October 22 and runs until November 13.
