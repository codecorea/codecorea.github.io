Fish fills the East Coast of winter

Gomchi soup, as its name suggests, is a fish called Gomchi. The name of this guy caught across the coast of Korea is slightly different from coast to coast. It is called Gomchi or Water Bear in the East Sea, Water Mage or Water Catfish in the South Sea, and Jambang or Water Tumbang in the West Sea. It can be interpreted that it is as common and popular that the word for the same fish varies from coast to coast. The most familiar name for us right now is water catfish and water squirrel, among which ‘water squirrel’ is the name that fishermen who caught gomchi threw ‘tumbles’ back into the water after seeing the appearance. The appearance of Gomchi, which can never be observed, can be seen in his nickname.
