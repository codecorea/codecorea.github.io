Star Park, Chilgapsan Observatory, facing the Sun
If you want to combine children's education and play on a fine day, visit the Chilgapsan Observatory Star Park located on the hillside of Chilgapsan. Opened in 2009 and operating as an astronomical themed science museum, it is more popular because it allows you to directly observe the sun as well as stars.
Korea's largest refractive telescope installed in the observation room
Observations take place in the main and secondary observation rooms on the third floor. In the observation room where the largest refractor is installed in Korea, sunspots and irises are observed. The secondary observation room, designed as a hemispherical sliding dome that surrounds the main observation room, observes the objects in the night sky such as planets, nebulae, and clusters at night. The astronomical projection room, where you can project constellations and celestial objects by projecting a virtual object like a real night sky on a 10m dome screen, is by far the most popular program that embodies the mystery of the universe in the hearts of children.
