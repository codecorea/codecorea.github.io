Hannam-dong Back Alley Tour

Yongsan-gu, Seoul, Korea

'Usadan-ro 10-gil', once located in Itaewon, once called the Liberation Village, has once again attracted the attention of the world. On the last Saturday of every month, we will visit the 10th Usadan-ro, Hannam-dong, a “warm town” in Seoul, which is being transformed into a forward base for small-business start-up as well as a space for artistic creation for young people.

Hansa-dong's Usadan-ro 10-gil, a young base for convergence and convergence of youth
Hansa-dong's Usadan-ro is less than 900 meters from Bogwang Elementary School to Cheongseok Super. Exit No. 3 of Itaewon Station (Line 6) and go up 100 meters to the '119 Safety Center' alley. Looking up from here, you can see two towers towering towards the sky. 'Seoul Central Masjid'. In front of the main entrance to the Islamic Masjid, the road that stretches in the direction of the Han River is Usadan.

Itaewon's famous staircase and “Come in store”
Here is a community that drives young desires and change. The young cultural artists 'Usadandan (雩 祀 壇 團)' combines interesting cultural contents and business with the young people traders. Since 2012, young people who live in the area and have business spaces such as workshops have been working together to create local culture and revitalize their business.
Stairs next to the central Islamic mosque where the staircase was held
The most representative business is the free market called 'stair leader'. The staircase, which started on 63 steps next to a mosque last spring, has only 30 to 40 participants, but now it has become one of the representative flea markets in Seoul.
