It is the 'President of Minui', but it is the place where the ordinary people cannot easily access. However, it is easy for anyone to visit and visit here if they want. However, if you make a reservation 3 days before the tour. A place that will be a good experience for adults as well as children's performance evaluation. In addition, it is closely related to the contents of the textbook, so in many ways it is worth calling it “the number one elementary field trip”.

Tours of the National Assembly are divided into the Constitutional Memorial Hall (the National Visitors Center) and the National Assembly. Previously, it was separate, but now it is possible to watch “one stop”. The Constitution Memorial Hall, which displays materials and artifacts related to the National Assembly of the Republic of Korea, can be freely visited, and the National Assembly Building must be gathered as a group at the time of reservation for guidance.
The dedication memorial hall is composed of the ‘Minister of the National Assembly’, ‘Temporary Assemblyman ’s Hall’, and ‘National Assembly Tour’. At the 'Temporary Court House', you can see the materials related to the provisional court and the first constitutional assembly, which is the legislative institution of the Korean Provisional Government. Upon receiving the guidance of the National Assembly guide, the House of Parliament listens to explanations of what the National Assembly is doing, and actually visits the main venue where the bill is being processed. The advantage is that all explanations are made to fit the eye level of the children. Rather than letting parents know something more by studying separately, it's a good idea to research the National Assembly with your child in advance and summarize your child's questions and ask the guide.

Then, let's go on a full-fledged tour of the National Assembly from now on. First, when you enter the entrance of the National Assembly, two Haitai greets visitors. Originally, Haitai is a legendary animal that judges fertility and good and evil. Because of this symbolism, it was a symbol of Saheungbu, an organization that inspects officials in the Joseon Dynasty. Perhaps the National Assembly stood in front of the National Assembly in the sense that the National Assembly would act on behalf of the people to monitor the government and correct the errors. Let's tell the children that this is the meaning of the name of the cookie maker.
