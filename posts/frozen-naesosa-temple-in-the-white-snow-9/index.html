King's beloved salt, resurrected in the modern day salt-bearing salt

After exiting Naesosa, climb National Route 30 and you will soon reach Gomso. Gomso has been famous for salt pans since ancient times. Gyehwabeol was the main production area, but during World War II, it became a land by constructing roads and building dikes linking the interlocking villages with Hodo (Bumseom), Ungyeon Island, and Sokdo during the end of the Japanese War. As such, the 45 hectares of Gomin-so-jeon are located in Gomso Port. The annual salt production boasts 2500 tons. Let's take a quick look at the signboard at the beginning of Gom Gomin.

Gomso's specialty is salt. In the past, salt was so important that it was managed by the state and must not have been sold until recently. One of the things to take to the king's refuge was salt. While man is necessary to live… (syncopation)… The production of salt in Buan was mainly flame, and the area of Gyehwa Daeverly was the main production area. Gomso's good salt taste makes fermented foods such as Gomso Salted Fish, Soybean Soybean Paste, and Cinnamic Bamboo Salt, a specialty.

Gomson solar salt is produced from March to the end of October. You can get salt every summer and every 3 to 5 days in spring and autumn. Summer salt is large in crystals and small in crystals but hard in autumn. Gommon sun salt has little bitter taste because it is not used when the concentration of liver water becomes dark.
