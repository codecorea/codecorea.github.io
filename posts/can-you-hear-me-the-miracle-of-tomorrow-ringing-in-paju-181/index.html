Can you hear me, the miracle of tomorrow ringing in Paju

Paju-si, Gyeonggi-do, Korea

In June 1950, the ground rang with a loud bombardment. Han River, Imjin River, Gongneung Stream, and Munsan Stream. Paju, which was called “the village on the bank,” had many roads in Oga. Today, 68 years later, Paju, arising from the pain of a dog-legged bridge, is a space that contains happiness, such as a museum and a garden cafe, touching people's wounds and dreaming of a miracle tomorrow.
Drawing a unification on the broken bridge, Skywalk
31 December 1950. The steam locomotive of Gyeongui Line, heading from North to South, was shot at 1020 rounds and stopped at Jangdan Station. The train, which had since been left in the DMZ, was not moved to Imjingak in 2006.
Steam locomotive at Jangdan Station on the Gyeongui Line, where it has been lost (Registered Cultural Property No. 78)
The Dokgae Bridge, an old railroad line connecting the old Gyeongui Line connecting Uncheon-ri, Munsan-eup, Paju-si and Nosang-ri, Jangdan-myeon, was also destroyed by bombing. From 1953 to 1998 when the Unification Bridge was opened from 1953, when the armistice was restored to allow the captives to return to the armistice, Dokgae Bridge, which was the only channel connecting the north of the civilian ship and Panmunjom, was a symbol of tragedy and pain. .
Imjingak served as the embankment of South Korea during the Korean War.
How to soothe the wounds that Ching Ching has wound. In December 2016, the dog's legs were refurbished in hopes that the scab would sit on the wind and hope for the hope to come together. Using the remaining five piers, the skywalk “Tomorrow's Miracle Sound”, which is 105m long and 5m wide, reproduces the iron bridge before the war. The two-story structure consists of the past, present, and future sections. Anyone can stay on Skywalk located within the civilian access control line without restrictions during the viewing time.
