Munrae-dong is a place where the relationship with the factory deepened as a spinning factory entered during the Japanese occupation. At that time, the spinning machine was called 'Mullae', and the place's name, 'Moonrae-dong' was said to have been established. Since then, steel factories and iron statues have been concentrated here, and now artists are flocking to this place, where art and ironworks coexist.

How did Moonrae-dong, the mecca of the steel industry, now become famous for the Moonrae Art Village? In the late 80s and early 90s, Munrae-dong was one of the most serious areas of air pollution in Korea. In line with this period, the city of Seoul attempted to move the steel sales districts around Munrae-dong and Dorim-dong to the outskirts. Since then, the steel industry in Munrae-dong has gradually declined and the number of vacancy has increased. Around the same time, artists who were looking for inexpensive work spaces began to fill the empty spaces here. Currently, there are about 100 workshops in Munrae-dong and about 200 artists are active, but the issue of redevelopment is said to be reducing the scope of the artist's activity as it reaches the Moonrae-dong.

Exit tips
1. It is good to use weekends and holidays when iron workshops and steel malls are closed. On the day off, you can see the pictures drawn on the shutters that fall down, and it's more suitable for weekends than on weekdays for leisurely walking around.
2. If you want to put a mural on a narrow alley, take a wide-angle lens. Smartphones with a panorama function are also useful.
3. You can access information related to artists' exhibitions and events at nearby cafes.
