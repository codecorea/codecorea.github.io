Youngdong Shenzhen-ri Village, the hometown of the heart when we miss the countryside

Yeongdong-gun, Chungbuk, South Korea

"I want to go back!" There are times when anyone wants to go somewhere, like the lines in a movie. At any point in the past, or anywhere in the past, there are times when you will be reminded of your memories. Especially when a big holiday is over. When the word family and home come to mind, busy hands are slowly turning upside down.
A simple station where memories live and the town scenery spread out like a black and white photo
But going back in time is not easy. As time goes by, the appearance of hometown is changing. In the meantime, there are some places that retain their former appearances as if they were passing away. If you want to go back to a moment in the past, take a slow train to Shenzhen station in Yeongdong, Chungbuk.
Shenzhen Station is a small convenience station. The station was opened as a regular station in 1905. The station has been newly built and moved to Gyeongbu Line double track construction in 1934. The history of the 1930s was preserved in the original form of the gable roof and was designated as Registered Cultural Property No. 297 in 2006. When you get off at the small station, you can see the green roof and the words 'the station where the old memories stay'. There are not many people getting on the train and getting off. The scent of dull feelings abounds in history and railroad tracks. The waiting room is small too.
"Once there were a lot of passengers and bustling times. The market was activated and many people gathered, but now it's a very quiet station." The station worker looks at the empty waiting room and explains the old look of Shenzhen Station.
Simple Shenzhen Station Waiting Room
Exiting the waiting room leads to the station square. The square is quite wide compared to the small train station. It is not only the station that Shenzhen has gone through.

It doesn't take long to get around the neighborhood around Shenzhen Station. If you want to spend more time here, take a bike hire from Shenzhen station. It is also known as 'blue bike'. Passengers traveling to Shenzhen on a train can travel for free. You can ride a bicycle along the nearby river or explore the nearby attractions.
If you visit Shenzhen by train, you can use the bicycle for free.
One course (8.1km) to Okgye Falls, two courses (9.4km) to Iwon Bridge, and three courses (5.8km) to Yeongdong Gugak Village.
Yeongdong Shenzhen-myeon, North Chungcheong Province is famous for the hometown of Nanji Park.
Now that we came to Shenzhen-myeon, Yeong-dong, Chungbuk, which is the hometown of the Nangye Park Yeon, one of the three malice in Korea, let's head to the Yeongdong Gugak Village. Yeongdong Gugak Village has Cheongogak along with facilities such as performance halls and experience rooms. The Cheongogak is the largest drum in the world, and is the space where the Cheongo is registered. The ceiling is 7 tons in weight, with a diameter of 5.54m, a diameter of 6.4m and a width of 5.96m.
Yeongdong Gugak Village, a free performance of Korean traditional music every Saturday
If you want to make full use of Yeongdong Gugak Village, visit on Saturday. This is because the permanent performance of Nanji Gugak Orchestra is held every Saturday. You can enjoy a variety of genres, from authentic Korean traditional music to fusion. During the performance, a description of Korean musical instruments may be given. There is also a space where you can touch the lyre, gayageum, and janggu at the entrance. After listening to the Korean traditional music performances, we will deal with musical instruments. The Korean traditional music, which felt difficult and far away, is approaching intimately.
You can play lyre, gayageum, and janggu yourself.
