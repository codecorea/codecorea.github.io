[Day Trip to Island] To you who have forgotten summer, Trekking Heaven Jangbongdo

Ongjin-gun, Incheon, Korea

Large and small peaks stood on the sea. Haemu came in and wrapped around comfortably. Gaetti-gil along the ridge has a fresh summer scent. Every time a blade of grass grazes the ankle, the cicadas are rumbling. I stopped walking for a moment and wiped the sweat from my forehead. I sat on a rock and watched the plane take off from Yeongjongdo Island and drank ice water. So I learned how to fly in Jangbongdo in the summer.
A light day trip to the island
Although travel is an everyday age, island travel still leads to the field of enthusiasts. This is because it takes a long time to reach the granularity, making it difficult to travel on a day, and there are many unpredictable variables due to the isolated environment.
Tourist information center, cafe, and tourist center
Nevertheless, the one-day island tour has been actively conducted around Incheon Ongjin-gun. Shin, Si, Modo, Jangbongdo, and Muuidodo, which are close to Yeongjongdo, are typical.
Seagull yawning near the dock
Among them, Jangbong-do is famous for a day-trip trekking spot. The name means `` island with long peaks, '' but there are seven trekking courses along the ridge and coast. Each course has different charms and features, including a fresh course on the 1st course from the marina to the middle of the island, as well as the sky path, the road beyond Gugi, the road to Jangbong Coast, the road to Yadal Mermaid, the path to Handeul Coast, and the path to Jangbong Treasure.
A small rocky island in front of Ongam Village.
Except for the 1st and 2nd courses across the central part of the island, they are all coastal walks along the sea. The sea seen from here is incredibly clean and blue, not to be believed to be the West Sea. At this time, most of the daytime, more of the low tide and tidal flats are revealed, but even this is just beautiful. The sandy tidal flat where the tidal flat meets the tidal rock is called ‘Got Tea’. This is the reason why the name “Got Ti-gil” was named on the trekking course of Jangbong-do.
It is called a remote place because it cannot go as far as it is near.
Twenty-five (one-way) flights a day also allow for a one-day trip. From Yeongjongdo Sammok Wharf, there are one or two ships heading to Jangbong-do almost every hour from 7:10 am to 8:40 pm. It takes about 40 minutes to depart from Sammok and arrive at Jangbongdo via Shindo. Passenger cars and freight cars are also available. Most people take the village bus from the marina and get off at the final stop, Chukdong (Jangbong 4-ri / Geoneojang Beach), and then walk along the Jangbong Coastal Road to Gamakmeori, the western end of the island.

When you return, you follow the ridge path, Heavenly Path, to Jinchon (Jangbong 3-ri), opposite the axis. This is a 3 hour and 30 minute course. You have to make a choice here.

If you want to go back to the marina by using the town bus, you need to go a little further to the starting point, Chuk-dong, and if you want to lack momentum, you can continue trekking along the fresh course of Course 1, which leads to the marina. In this case, it takes 6-7 hours.

Observation deck ban is forbidden, so if you spend your time, you will wander in the dark. There are many sunset points around the village, such as the dried fish beach and Yadal dock, so there is no need to be sad.
Stamp post installed on the observation deck.

A trekking paradise is not a smooth road. There are few decks or stairs and most of them are made of dirt and rocks, which can be difficult for those unfamiliar with climbing. In addition, grass and trees are lush when there is a shower in the summer, making some areas uncomfortable. The fresh play path and the sky path (under difficulty) stretching along the main ridgeline are relatively wide and flat, but the Jangbong Coastal Path (on difficulty level) is narrow and rough, so it is recommended to avoid trekking beginners as much as possible. Even if it is not a coastal road, keep in mind that Jangbongdo itself has long ridges and repeats the uphill and downhill paths, so it is easy to get tired even if the course is short.

Jangbongdo's Hidden Treasure
① Beach with tidal flats
There are 5 beaches in Jangbong-do. Ongam Beach, the closest to the marina, is a representative tourist attraction with various restaurants and convenient facilities. On weekends, camping crowds are so crowded that the field is filled with tents. The wide tidal flat is a playground for children to play freely. 뙤 It's busy picking up shellfish or crabs without worrying about the sun. Jangbongdo is a mudflat everywhere. In the distant sea, there are several places of grass. In some places, the depths are deep and the water is fast. It was called the golden fishing ground from the Joseon Dynasty for a good reason.
Quiet and quiet weekday afternoon Ongam Beach
There are also mermaid legends related to abundant fishery resources. The fisherman Choi Choi, who hit the ball every day, was delighted to find a mermaid caught in the net one day, but the eyes seemed so sad that he put it back to the sea, and since then, every time a fisherman throws a net, a fish is caught. In fact, you can meet a mermaid statue built at the dock in Jangbongdo to remember the mermaid's boeun.
Sister picking something up from tidal flat
② Delicious seafood
Sanghap, Sora, and clams are seafood representing Jangbongdo. The elderly in the village go out near the grass and offshore in small streets to catch up. Mussels are lilies belonging to the most expensive species of shellfish. It is caught a lot in the nearby tidal flats, but the bigger and better the competition is, the farther it goes out to sea. Sora is the same. It comes from the tidal flats and from the sea, and the mussel is bigger and tastes better. The collected sashimi is purchased at the village restaurant. Mussels and clams are kalguksu, and sora is served in bibimbap.
Sorbibimbap at the “Sky Garden” restaurant, which boasts a colorful color
Sora Bibimbap, jointly developed by Incheon City and Cheongwoon University, is a representative menu of Jangbongdo. The fat seashell was full. Even the first chapter contains the philosophy and spirit of the food maker. The ‘Sky Garden’ plant is sweet and not spicy with apples, plums, and pears. For customers who don't have a good stomach, they make instant soy sauce.
