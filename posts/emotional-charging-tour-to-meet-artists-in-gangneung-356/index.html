The Oriental Embroidery Museum displays high-quality traditional embroidery works and props from three countries: Korea, China, and Japan.

The 2018 PyeongChang Winter Olympics and Gangneung were held on ice. There is a Gangneung Olympic Museum near Gyeongpoho where you can feel the excitement of the last Pyeongchang Olympics again. The exhibition hall, composed of five themes, recycles used containers to capture the meaning of the Environment Olympics. The container is painted in blue, red, yellow, green, and black in accordance with the color of the five-wheel mark, and is lightly bright.
Gangneung Olympic Museum
The Olympics compete for ranking in a total of 15 events, 7 events in snow, 3 events in sliding, and 5 events in ice. Dynamic shaped sculptures that represent each item attract attention. The equipment attached to the sculptures such as sticks, goggles, and helmets is more realistic by using what the national team actually used.
At the 4D Experience Center, you can watch videos by selecting a few of the events such as ski jumping, bobsleigh, snowboarding, and alpine skiing. When the ski lands on the slope, the chair shakes and feels the wind, creating an illusion that you are actually playing. Experience the ski jump in VR (virtual reality), and it is also vividly unfolded in front of you. The facility is also fun, allowing visitors to experience curling matches by throwing stones. There are many things to see, including Gyeongpo Aquarium, Gangneung Green City Experience Center, and Heo Gyun and Heonan Seolsaeng Birthplace.

The Charmsori Gramophone & Edison Science Museum is a collection of representative inventions of the phonograph and Edison. It has more Edison phonographs than the Edison Museum in the United States. Of the 4,000 phonographs made in over 30 countries, 3,500, 150,000 records, and 5,000 music-related books are packed with music, so it's hard to see which one is important. You can look around individually, but it is much more enjoyable to listen to the exhibition commentary as the professional staff will tell you about the exhibits and episodes.

Light Bulb Invented by Edison
It is divided into the Charmsori Gramophone Museum and the Edison Science Museum. The phonograph museum is divided into 1 exhibition hall featuring a trumpet phonograph, 2 exhibition halls with cabinet-type phonographs, 3 exhibition halls mainly exhibited by music boxes, and radio and TV exhibition halls. A trumpet gramophone with a large trumpet shaped like a flower is often associated with a phonograph. There are a lot of things that look good when you put on a record and connect it to a power source, as if beautiful music would be played right away.
The volume of collections such as gramophones and music boxes is enormous.
The Edison Science Museum is a collection of exhibitions on light, collections on sound, and household items and inventions. After many trials and errors, Edison's light bulb is one of the most important inventions in human history. The number of light bulbs on display, including the first carbon light bulbs, is about 500. Without Edison, housework would have been much more intense than it is now. Coffee pots, toasters, waffles, ovens, irons, sewing machines, mixers, microwave ovens, and hair dryers are said to have been invented by Edison for his wife. The end of the tour ends with sitting in comfortable seats and listening to beautiful music. In the building next door, there is Son Sung-mok Movie, Radio, and TV Museum with a variety of movie-related collections.

The mission field was built by Narayon, the eleventh generation of Hyoryeongdaegun (the elder brother of King Sejong the Great), and is a high-class house in the Joseon Dynasty, with the typical appearance of a father-in-law. It has been preserved in its original form without damage to the teenagers over 300 years. It is said that Nae-Bun, who was looking for a home, followed a weasel and fell in love with an early forest.

traditional market
-Gangneung Central Market
Gangneung Jungang Market is a traditional market representing Yeongdong area. It is divided into a traditional market located on the first and second floors above the ground and an underground fish market, and the fish market is full of fresh seafood from the East Sea. In addition to the fish market, there are a variety of stores including vegetables, grains, industrial products, side dishes, and daily necessities. Across the market, Eatja Alley is a representative food of Gangneung such as buckwheat pancake, potato onion, red bean porridge, and potato jeon.
