The mystery of the millennium, revealing the secret of the birth of the eighteen thousand blacksmiths ‘Hapcheon Blacksmith Theme Park’

Hapcheon-gun, Gyeongnam, Korea

The Tripitaka Koreana is considered as one of the greatest relics throughout Korea's history, or even Korea's history. Although there are about 20 species of blacksmith in the world, the eighteen thousand blacksmiths are listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in June 2007 due to their high accuracy and completeness. What is so great about the eighteen thousand colonel? The place where you can see with your eyes, touch with your hands, and dig into the excellence of the blacksmith is the theme park. The entire process, from the production motives, processes, and storage of the eighteen thousand blacksmiths, can be systematically known, which not only helps children experience learning, but also allows adults to feel the mystery of the blacksmiths once again.

The Blacksmith Theme Park is, as its name suggests, the theme of the eighteen thousand blacksmiths. I know only that the country and the people suffering from the Mongol invasion were made to save by the power of the Buddha, but the deeper and more mysterious the world, the closer it is to the world of the Blacksmith.

The first floor of the blacksmith exhibition room
After passing through the theme park entrance and passing through the neatly formed square and park, you will reach Daejanggyeong Millennium Hall, the main exhibition space. The circular exhibition hall on the first floor, which penetrates to the ceiling, is filled with hardwood plates. The fact is that the image of Haeinsa Jang Kyung Panjeon is reflected on the wall. Also displayed is the Dongpan Blacksmith, which was produced to permanently preserve the Palmman Blacksmith, a wooden board.
The appearance of the coppersmith

On the second floor, there are the Blacksmith Road Room, Mystery Room, Conservation Science Room, and Understanding Room. The Blacksmith Road Room introduces the process of recording and disseminating the Blacksmith.

Daejanggyeong Mystery Room shows all these processes in an easy-to-understand way. In addition, there is a space where you can find out about the eighteen thousand colonoscopes through numbers. For example, the total length of the eight thousand and eighteen thousand pieces is 8,1,258, and a single plate is 70 cm wide, 24 cm long, and 2.8 cm thick, stacked in a row, 508 m higher than Baekdusan. The translation work took a total of 36 years, the gross weight of the plate was about 280 tons, 108 pillars in the Jangyeong Panjeon, and 0 of the corroded plates in the large colon ... . It expresses the greatness of the blacksmith who was vaguely known in numbers, so it easily touches the skin.
Blacksmith in numbers
In addition to the eighteen thousand colonel, the Jangkyung Panjeon, which stores the plates, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Preservation Science Lab reveals the principles of science hidden in the Jang Kyeong Pan. You can check the location of Jangyeongpanjeon, considering the altitude of the sun, the size and location of the window that facilitates ventilation, and the soil with charcoal, sashimi, salt and sand on the floor to control humidity and prevent pests.

You can't come here and go back without seeing Haeinsa Temple. Haeinsa Temple, which possesses the eighteen thousand colonel, is one of the three treasures in Korea, along with Tongdosa Temple, and Songkwangsa Temple, the Buddhist temple. Founded in the 3rd year of King Silla (802), its history is over a thousand years old. On the outer wall of the main hall, Daejeokjeon, there is a mural depicting the story of Haeinsa Temple.
