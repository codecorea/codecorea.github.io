A feast of beautiful light to enjoy in winter, Gapyeong Morning Calm Arboretum

Gapyeong-gun, South Korea

On the way to the Moonlight Garden past the hometown garden, a long tunnel that looks like hanging pink petals awaits. As if you are just starting to love, in the pink tunnel, pink smile flowers bloom on the faces of lovers. The entrance from the morning square to the Moonlight Garden is like a fairy tale country. Giant bears and mushrooms, all sorts of animals and a sled-pulling Rudolph sit amongst the larch. The white church with an angel inside is the most popular photo zone.
Moonlight Garden Scene with white church
The moonlight garden that leads to the white church revolves around a larch tree with light like white vines, reminiscent of the fairy tale Jack and the Beanstalk.
White church popular as a photo zone of the Moonlight Garden
The beauty of the Five Star Garden, the morning square and the Hagyeong Garden
When descending from the moonlight garden along the sky path, it leads to the morning square with fine green grass. Here, a golden carriage going to the Starlight Palace stands against a blue light. It is a popular photo zone for lovers and families to stand in line with a white church to take pictures.
Wagon standing in the morning square
Blue lights twinkle behind the golden carriage. It feels like a milky way, not a universe in the dark.
Blue light in the morning square resembling the Milky Way
Hagyeong Garden is a main garden of the Morning Calm Garden and a garden resembling the Korean Peninsula. It is also a place where you can meet the most beautiful color and harmony of light in the Five Star Light Garden.
Scenery from Hagyeong Garden South Observatory
The Hagyeong Garden has observation decks on the east, west, and south sides, and you can overlook the spectacular view of the Hagyeong Garden.
Scenery from Hagyeong Garden Observatory
The observatory is a crowd of people who want to take a commemorative photo or capture a beautiful night scenery against the backdrop of a colorful garden.
Hakyung Garden also has trees decorated like Christmas trees.
You can explore the charm of the Five-Colored Starlight Festival by looking around the Hagyeong Garden as well as the observation deck. The light of the tree is illuminated and the garden is projected.
People enjoying the night view of Hakyung Garden
Visually, it overwhelms the most spectacular scenery in spring or autumn. The color of the primary colors created by the colorful lights has a strange charm that makes it easy to stay.
Large flowers give a gorgeous night view
There are not many people who fall into the beauty of Hagyeong Garden and the morning square and go to Seohwayeon, a traditional pond garden.
Pine trees with lights in the shape of trees
The scenery of Seohwayeon overlooking the traditional teahouse Seohwa-ga is also indispensable. A pink rose blooms in a magnificent view of a peacock tree standing by the pond.
Hakyung Garden surrounded by the enchanting light
