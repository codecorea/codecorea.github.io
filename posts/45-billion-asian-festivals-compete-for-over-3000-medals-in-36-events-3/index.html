4.5 billion Asian festivals! Compete for over 3000 medals in 36 events

Seo-gu, Incheon, South Korea

On September 19, the 17th Incheon Asian Games started with the opening ceremony of Incheon Asiad Main Stadium in Incheon, Korea. 45 Asian countries, including Korea, China, and Japan, compete for over 3000 medals in 36 sports (28 Olympics and 8 Olympics) including basketball, rugby, archery, weightlifting, swimming, canoeing and boxing. The event, which includes all members of the Asian Olympic Council (OCA), including North Korea, will be held for 16 days until October 4. The third Asian Games to be held in Korea is the third after Seoul in 1986 and Busan in 2002. As of 2014, only Thailand (4) and South Korea have hosted more than three Asian Games.

Departure begins at the opening ceremony of Incheon Asiad Main Stadium on September 19, 2014

It boasts a floor area of 11,620m2 (approximately 34,000 pyeong) and five floors and more than 60,000 seats. The competition started with the opening ceremony of Incheon Asiad Main Stadium. Wait here! There are two types of archery: a recurve bow and a compound bow. All archery we have seen in the meantime is recurve. Compound archery hasn't been an Olympic or Asian competition. However, attention has been increasing as it was adopted as a regular sport since the Incheon Asian Games. What is the difference between the two? As the name implies, the bow structure is different. If the recurve is a traditional bow, the compound is a mechanical bow. The recurve is heavy and sensitive, so the player's concentration is important. Thinking of the archery field in the meantime makes it easier to understand. Compounds, on the other hand, are lighter and smaller than mechanical arrowhead recurve arrows that are easy to learn for beginners with pulleys and sights.
