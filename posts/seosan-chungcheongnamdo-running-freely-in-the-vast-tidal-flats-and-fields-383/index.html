Starry Night, Seosan-ryu Bangtaek Astronomical Meteorological Museum
It is too early to turn to the hostel. It's time to see the stars and shining planets that fill the night sky of Seosan.
Seosan Ryubang Astronomical Meteorological Museum
Seosan Ryubangtaek Astronomical Meteorological Museum is not well known, but if you go there, you will be surprised by Ryu Bangtaek's achievements.
Photo zone at the entrance
Let's open the back of the ten thousand won bill in your wallet. If you look closely at the background pattern, you can see the map of the celestial train field, the main astronomical map of Joseon, behind Honcheon. This map, designated as National Treasure No. 228, was produced by a teacher named Geum Heon Ryu Bang-taek from Seosan, Chungnam. It is the second oldest in the world to depict the entire sky, and it is the first Jeoncheon Seokgakdo to express its size differently according to the brightness of the stars. You can learn more in the exhibition room on the first floor, which is free of charge.
The Big Dipper of the Heavenly Train Field Map
The first floor consists of an astronomical projection room, an exhibition education room, and a chromakey experience space. You can observe the sunspots and red flames of the ocean and various astronomical objects in the main observation room and auxiliary observation room on the second floor.
Auxiliary observation room
Admission is 3,000 won for adults and children under 6 are free. It can be used from 2pm to 10pm during the winter season (September to April), and from 3pm to 11pm during the summer season (May to August). However, since daily observations vary depending on the weather, check by phone before visiting.
1st floor exhibition hall
Again At Park Myeong hours (17: 30 ~ 19: 30), neither the stars nor the sun can be observed, so you can only see the permanent exhibition hall. Infants under the age of 36 months are not admitted to the astronomical observation room and the astronomical projection room.
