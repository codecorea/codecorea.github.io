Andong is said to have originated in Changnyeongguk, founded by Yeomsang Dosa in 57 BC. It is called Gotaya-gun and Gochang-gun in Silla era. Afterwards, he entered the Joseon Dynasty and was promoted to Andong-do. Daedohobu is the name of a local administrative agency that was placed in the main town of the Joseon Dynasty. In addition to Andong, it was installed in Gangneung, Pyeongan, Yeongbyeon, and Hamgyeong, Yeongheung. In 1896 (Gojong 33), Andong-gun was passed to Andong-eup in 1931. In 1995, the Andong-gun was merged into Andong-si. This is the reason why it has the largest area among the nation's counties.

Andong Travel NO.1 Poongsan Ryu's Zipseong Village Hahoe Village
It is 5.7km from Gyeongsangbuk-do, Gyeonggi-do and Hahoe Village in 10 minutes by car. Hahoe Village, a 600-year-old village of Pungsan Ryu, flows in the 'S' shape with the Nakdong River surrounding the village. This is why the village name became 'Hahoe'. In feng shui geography, it is 'soft softening type' which is considered as the best road. It resembles a lotus floating on water. It is famous for being a good place for people to live in since Joseon Dynasty because there are mountains and rivers centering on villages. In July 2010, it was listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Byeongsan Seowon is devoted to the study in harmony with nature
Passing the volcano (271m) from Hahoe Village, it is Byeongsan Seowon. The beautiful Seowon, which overlooks the flowing stems of the Nakdong River, began in 1575 when Seo-ae Ryu Sung-ryong moved Pungak Seodang in Pungsan-eup. Ryu Sung-ryong's pupils and larvae set up his shrine here and became a formal vow. It is one of 47 vows and shrines that received the sum of 'Byeongsan' in 1863 and survived the Seowon Abolition of Heungseondaewon.
