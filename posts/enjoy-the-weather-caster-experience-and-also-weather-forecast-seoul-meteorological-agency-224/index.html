How is the weather in Korea this summer? Let's take a look at the three-month forecast from the Korea Meteorological Administration from June to August 2013. “The summer temperature will be higher than the average, and the precipitation will be similar to the average. 9 to 11 typhoons will occur, and 1 to 2 will affect Korea. ” If you look at this in detail by month, “There will be many cloudy days under the influence of low pressure in July and many hot days under August under the influence of high pressure in the North Pacific.” Families interested in weather forecasting and meteorological phenomena should visit the Korea Meteorological Administration and enjoy experiential learning. Like a weather forecaster, you can draw a weather map based on the air pressure layout and stand in front of a TV camera to become a weather caster that delivers weather news.

As soon as you enter the front door of the Japan Meteorological Agency, you can see the right and left sides. In 1441, during the time of King Sejong in Joseon, a rain gauge to measure rainfall was invented. Previously, it was said to measure the depth of rainwater that had penetrated the ground with a homina plow. The rain gauge displayed on the porch of the Korea Meteorological Administration is a reproduction of the gold rain gauge (treasure No. 561), which was produced in 1837 and used in Gongyeong Gamyeong (Geumyeong). The authentic product was taken out to the Japan Meteorological Agency during the Japanese colonial rule, and was returned in 1971. It is divided into three stages, and it is a bamboo ruler called ‘Jukcheok’.
The sideband refers to the side stand. There are two exhibits on the porch, one of which is the Gwanwoodae (Treasure No. 843), which was in a sense of contemplation. Gwansanggam is the government office in charge of astronomical, geographic, and posthumous affairs in the Joseon Dynasty.
Large pictures of Sejong's servants and their raindrops on the side of the raincoat and the raincoat catch the visitors' attention. The spirit of Sejong's love for the people seems to be transmitted vividly in the 21st century.

The Meteorological Agency experience learning is conducted in the order of watching the Meteorological Agency video, drawing a weather map, visiting observation equipment, experiencing weather casters, and exploring outdoor observation equipment. On the first day of each month, you can apply for experiential learning for the next month. Individual experiences are held every Wednesday, and group experiences are held every Thursday and Friday. Online reservations are only available through the Japan Meteorological Agency website. The duration of the experience is about 2 hours.
First, we gather in the auditorium to find out what the Korea Meteorological Administration is doing through animation. Cartoon protagonists tell the process of weather forecasting without boring. It is conducted in the order of current weather identification, data collection, analysis, forecasting, and notification. Of course, a detailed explanation of each process follows. Also, find out the meaning of the sign that appears in the weather map. The parents who accompanied the children also miss a word and focus on their mind.

The symbols in the weather map have their own meaning. It means the amount of clouds, the direction of the wind, the speed of the wind, the current weather, and air pressure. In the final stage of drawing the weather map, it is necessary to complete the shape of air pressure bones such as low pressure and high pressure.
I need to know common sense about air pressure. The force that the air surrounding the Earth presses down by gravity is called air pressure. It is Italian physicist Torricelli who expresses the pressure in numbers. He experimented with filling a 1m glass tube with one end clogged with mercury and standing upside down in a mercury container. At this time, it was determined that one mercury column corresponds to a height of about 760 mm. The unit of air pressure is hectopascal (hPa).

After watching the video for about an hour, the children try to draw a diary. In the old days, it was unpredictable to see rain or drought. So, when there was a severe drought, even the king went out and stayed in the sky. How is it today? In order to protect people's lives and property, the Korea Meteorological Administration provides all kinds of scientific equipment such as supercomputers and meteorological satellites to provide weather information, typhoon information, and weather information. Also, in order to support the people's life and industrial activities, they regularly give out local and weekly forecasts, long-term forecasts, and even mountain and airport weather.
Weather forecasting is to grasp the change of weather from the past to the present and use it to predict how the weather in the future will change. Observe air temperature, air pressure, wind, humidity, precipitation, and clouds in a variety of ways, including automatic weather observation equipment, satellites, radar, ships, and aircraft. It analyzes the data collected not only in Korea but also around the world and draws a weather map. Based on this weather map, experienced weather forecasters deliver comprehensive weather forecasts to the public.
Weather forecasting has a great impact on our lives, our environment, and our industry. If you have a rain forecast, you can pack an umbrella when you leave the house. When the weather forecast that the sun will be bright will come out, the car wash is full. At industrial sites, we listen very closely to weather forecasts. Depending on the weather forecast for the upcoming season, you will be making a lot of winter clothes, increasing the production of air conditioning appliances, or adjusting the production of ice cream.
