#One. Uljin Wangpicheon, full of wild charm
Wangpicheon is a 61km-long waterway that flows from Wangpi-ri and Gusan-ri to Uljin, originating from the nutritional defense of Gyeongbuk. Surrounded by mountains and cliffs, it was not easy to reach, so people were less likely to walk, and thanks to it, it was considered a clean area to recognize. The name 'Wangpiri', meaning 'town where the king has fled', also indicates that this area was remote. It is in the same context that Wangpicheon area was designated as an ecological and landscape conservation area in 2005. It is the largest in Korea. In 2013, four ecological trails were opened in the vicinity of Wangpicheon and designated as eco-tourism areas, where local residents operate the Wangpicheon Eco Tour Group.
Wolmi, Yongso of Uljin Wangpicheon, one of the best places in the world <Photo courtesy, Uljin County Office>
The starting point of this Wangpicheon trekking course was taken to Gulguji Village. Gulguji Village, which is considered to be an outback in Uljin, is a name that says that only nine bends should go around the mountain. The trekking route between Wangpicheon Soksa Village, upstream of Wangpicheon and Gulguji Village, downstream of Gusan-ri, is the most successful. Either way, you can start, but if you are a beginner or a child with little trekking experience, it is recommended that you start at Goolji Village and return to the camp. The valley is gentle and you can trek gently.

If you are walking in the valley and become crowded, you can also climb the forest path. Still, trekking in the valley is recommended to properly see the skin of Wang Picheon. # 3. Deep mountain valley road, Jeongseon Deoksangi Valley
Deoksangi Valley, one of Jeongseon's remote areas. It was a secret valley known only to people who knew it until it was introduced to TV programs. It was revealed to the world when it was chosen as a place for outdoor weddings by famous celebrities, but the still waters still remain.
With a total length of 12km, there are Dosagok, Deoksangi, and Hebei-dong villages in the valley of Deoksangi, which is surrounded by Songdae. Dozens of households lived on the ground and left for flooding in 1979. Guest houses and pensions are located along the valley. In general, we find many courses from Deokuri to Budong-ri.
Deoksangi Valley, Jeongseon, one of the remote areas
Those staying in a pension or guest house in the Deoksangi Valley should park and walk in the Deoksan Bridge. The cement road continues for the villagers living in the valley of Deoksangi. A proud stand rising by the gentle stream greets you. To enjoy Deoksangi Valley trekking properly, it is better to go after rain.
