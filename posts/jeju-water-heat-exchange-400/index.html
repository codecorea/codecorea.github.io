Looking at the dictionary meaning of water sashimi, 'freshly picked fish or squid into pieces finely' is 'food that is mixed with seasoning such as green onion, garlic, and red pepper and poured with water'. It is a food developed usually in the sea, and is commonly found in Jeju and other coastal areas such as Pohang and Sokcho.
In particular, in Jeju, where the slope is surrounded by the sea, water sashimi was a wise recipe to eat fish or seafood, the main ingredients, even in summer. The main condiments of water, garlic, vinegar, and peeled leaves, have sterilizing effects, and have improved the preservation of ingredients, and the sour sauce with vinegar also played a role in regaining the lost taste in the heat. This is the reason why water inevitably developed in Jeju. Even now, in Jeju, any restaurant or water sashimi is the most popular in summer.
Traditional Jeju water sashimi has a sour taste using fresh leg vinegar made by fermenting raw miso and barley rice in a fresh taste. Add garlic, green pepper, and cuticle leaves, chopped to taste, to taste. If you are unfamiliar with Jeju-style indigenous sauce, this traditional style of water may feel a bit greasy. Moreover, because of the popularity of land-style water soybean paste made with red pepper paste, it is more popular in Jeju today than in traditional methods.
