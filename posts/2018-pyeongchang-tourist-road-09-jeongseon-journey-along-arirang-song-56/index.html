"Ari and Ari and Ariari. Ari and pass me over your head." Arirang melodies are melted in Jeongseon. Arirang, which is called at the lowest point of life, is intimately attached to our lives and narrates love, farewell, coalition, and lament. The vibrancy of life at the Jeongseon Arirang Market and the sound of the river flowing with the rail bike are like the rhythm of Arirang. Step on the rail bike pedals to follow Arirang's faults and ride a straw wire to Jeongseon, where you can embrace the Korean peninsula.
Journey starts at Auraji, the birthplace of Arirang. Auraji is named after Songcheon and Goljicheon. The two fabrics combine to form a river from the Auraji, the highest point of the Han River. When you enter Arari Village, you will find a house that recreates traditional life such as traditional waga, oyster house, nawa house, and watermill. It is not a museum where you can touch it, but it is an experience facility where you can experience pottery, crafts, and hands-on experiences at each house.

Byeongchi Chi-Skywalk is an observatory suspended from a cliff at 583 meters above sea level, which is twice the height of Yeouido 63 Building. It is a place where Donggang winds around the mountain and has a view of the night islands shaped like the Korean peninsula. It is made of transparent tempered glass, so you can enjoy the feeling of walking while floating on the scenery under your feet. Although tempered glass can be dangerous if people are too crowded, limit the number of people sent in.
