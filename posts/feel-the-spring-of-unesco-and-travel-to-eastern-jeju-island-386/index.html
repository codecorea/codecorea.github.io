Feel the spring of UNESCO and travel to eastern Jeju Island
In March. With the beginning of spring, flowers begin to bloom on Jeju Island. The unique charm of volcanic islands that cannot be seen on land leads to Jeju's footsteps in Jeju.
Seongsanpo JC Park & Rape Flower Growing Complex
Rape flowers are the first flowers to announce the spring of Jeju Island. Seongsanpo JC Park, called Gwangchigi, is a sunrise spot, and people's footsteps continue from dawn to dawn. Many people have gone through madness, and the sand on the beach is hardened, so you can use your wheelchair to move carefully on the sand.
Sunrise at Seongsanpo JC Park
Beside Seongsan Ilchulbong, there is a rapeseed cultivation complex with yellow buds behind the sun rising in the red. In March, it is busy to take photos between the rapeseed field. On the other hand, there is a rape flower field that is accessible by wheelchair. It is a rapeseed flower field that requires a wheelchair to enter, although it has to go through a slightly rugged dirt road. In the early morning, go to Seongsanpo JC Park and rapeseed cultivation complex.
Spring in Jeju starting with rape flowers
Jeju Folk Village
About 30 minutes by car from Seongsanpo JC Park is Jeju Folk Village near the starting point of Jeju Olle 4 Course. It is a huge outdoor museum that reproduces the life of Jeju in the 19th century volcanic island. There is a hanbok rental booth near the main entrance where the fragrances of Cheonri and Manri are spread.
Jeju Folk Village, famous for filming the drama <Daejanggeumui>
Folk villages are flat enough to move the wheelchair. You can also hear the sound of stepping on a square sand on a solid dirt road. Go to Gwana after passing through mountain village, middle mountain village, and fishing village. Steep ramps require a little help.

Jeju Olle-gil 4 course with Kim Tae-hee Lighthouse
Right next to Jeju Folk Village, follow the starting point of Jeju Olle-gil 4 course. From Pyoseon Haevichi Hotel and Resort to Gamarigae Shelter, about 4.8 km is the Olle-gil 4-course wheelchair section. One white lighthouse stands out in the middle of the wheelchair section of Jeju Olle-gil 4-course.
Lighthouse at the starting point of Jeju Olle-gil 4 course

Jeju Haenyeo's Life and Love Jeju Haenyeo Museum
You can see Jeju's human heritage, recognized by the world, at the Jeju Haenyeo Museum. Currently, there are less than 4,000 haenyeo in Jeju Island, but the life of Haenyeo, the leading figure in Jeju, is preserved as an intangible cultural property.
Way to Haenyeo Museum
When you enter the Jeju Haenyeo Museum, the first time you watch an 8-minute video on the theme of Haenyeo culture in the video exhibition room. In the video exhibition room, there are no wheelchair seats. However, the wheelchair can be viewed from the frontmost seat where the screen is most visible.
Haenyeo Museum 3rd Floor Observation Deck
Go to the 2nd floor of the museum following the cultural tourism commentator who resides in the museum. In order to dry the wet water buffalo (old haenyeo costume), the figure of a haenyeo is lit by the fire on the fire.

World-recognized geological attraction Gimnyeong Geological Trail
Gunyeong-eup, Gimnyeong-ri is a geological attraction recognized by World Natural Heritage. There are Geumnyeong and Woljeong geological trail courses based on geological attractions such as Manjanggul, Dangcheomulgul, Gimnyeonggul, and Yongcheondonggul. The general course takes 14.6km and takes 5 hours, while the short course takes 4.7km and takes an hour and a half, but it is not suitable for wheelchair travelers.
Gimnyeong Eoul Center, the starting point of Gimnyeong Geological Trail
Although it is not official, we will follow the wheelchair course, which is easy to access for the Kimnyeong area. Starting with the Gimnyeong Eoul Center, the starting point of the Geological Trail, the Gimnyeong Geological Trail goes to the Century Al Beach, Dodaebul, Intertidal Zone, Cheonggul Water, Gout Samgul and Gout Sammul, Health Ville Garden, Bonhyangdang, Seongseungdang, Jornville Trail and Gimnyeong Seongseung Beach This is a wheelchair course.
Century Beach with impressive jade sea
Gout Samgul and Gout Samgul are underground caves where Yongcheonsu leads to blue water, and the main hall and Seongseangdang have steep stairs, making wheelchair access difficult. However, it is possible to fully enjoy the geological trail of Gimnyeong even with the view of the emerald Century Al Beach and the blue water accessible by wheelchairs near the sea. Gimnyeong · Woljeong Geologic Trail is useful when you receive the <Jeju Geo> application.

Jeju Stone Jeju Stone Culture Park
Jeju Stone Culture Park is a historical and cultural park that integrates Jeju stone culture. Based on the survey questionnaire and the legend of General Baek Baek, you can get a glimpse into the story of the strangeness of Jeju volcanic rocks.
Jeju Stone Museum to know the types of volcanic rocks
Jeju Stone Culture Park Parking Lot No. 2, which is the closest to the ticket office, is convenient for people with disabilities. The lift bus parking lot for tourists with disabilities is particularly impressive. The first section from the ticket office to the Jeju Stone Museum shows the ecology of Gotjawal, but since steep slopes appear in the middle, assistance is needed when moving.
Lift for the disabled at the Jeju Stone Museum
Wheelchair access is very convenient from the sky pond to the Jeju Stone Museum. With the guidance of a tourist culture commentator, you will learn about the different types of volcanic rocks and their birth background. The lava stone displayed in the museum can be touched so that even the visually impaired can see the exhibition with touch.
Jeju Stone Museum Access Road
After leaving the museum, there are many volcanic clusters and gravel in the section from the outdoor exhibition hall to the exit.

Encounter with Mother Nature Jeolmul Natural Recreation Forest
At the end of winter, a yellow revenge herb that announces spring has blossomed in the Cedar Forest Jeolmul Natural Recreation Forest. In Jeolmul Natural Recreation Forest, there is a deck path that allows wheelchairs to move between straight cedar forests. The point of Jeolmul Recreation Forest is that you can enjoy the forest bath while feeling the phytoncide of the cedar forest.
A flare that signals the springtime
From the ticket office, the entrance to the cedar road is steep and the manual wheelchair is difficult to move alone. When you enter the entrance with the help of a companion, cedar trees stretch straight on both sides of the road. In the meeting forest, there is a deck path where you can walk between cedar trees. In the middle of the deck road passing by a wheelchair, there is a rest area or a ramp leading to the normal.
Samul-gil Road
The Jeolmul Natural Recreation Forest is equipped with a family-friendly handicap toilet, 'Beautiful Jeolmul Toilet', so anyone can use it conveniently.

Maze Landing on a Wheelchair Maze Land
Maze Land is located 22 km from Jeolmul Natural Recreation Forest. Taking advantage of the three characteristics of Samdado, Harbang, the woman, and the wind maze wait for the visitors. It's fun to find an exit through a maze that can't be seen outside. Follow the maze in a wheelchair. When I looked back because I couldn't find the exit, there were traces of wheelchair wheels on the dirt road in the maze. When you exit the maze, you can see an azimuthal path with orange lights twinkling. Gravel is on the unpaved dirt road and you have to move carefully.
Maze puzzle museum view from the bridge
There are many fun puzzle games at Mazeland Maze Puzzle Museum. Anyone, young or old, can stay in the museum for a while and enjoy a puzzle game. The complex shape of the labyrinth puzzle can be felt with a touch, so it can be enjoyed by the visually impaired.
Travelers enjoying the complex shape of the labyrinth puzzle
Take the elevator to the rooftop of the museum, and there is a observatory where you can see at a glance the Darangsi Oreum and three mazes. The observation deck is made of all glass so that people with low vision can enjoy the surrounding scenery, such as wheelchair users.

Let's all turn together, a round alley of mural village
Dumeni Alley is a travel destination in eastern Jeju that is not restricted by the tourist operating hours. In 2008, Jeju's unique lifestyle was painted as a mural through the alleyway regeneration project. Dolphin, turban, and installation art such as tawak, haenyeo, fish, and questionnaire are all illustrated.
More beautiful murals with stories
From the Gujungrodangdang, the starting point, a pink road sign is drawn at the bottom of the entire course. If you follow the displayed color so that you don't need a map, you can see every corner of the alley.
Two blind alleys with murals and installation art
However, there are many steep slopes in the two blind alleys, so it is difficult to move the manual wheelchair alone. This simple alley, perfect for traveling with friends and family, is filled with Jeju's mood.
Alley scenery where you can meet Jeju's unique lifestyle
