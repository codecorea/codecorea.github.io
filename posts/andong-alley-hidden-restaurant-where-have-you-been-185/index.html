Andong Geonjin Noodles cooked by a grandmother with red sesame seeds
Rumors are good, but the most difficult food to eat is Andong Geonjin Noodles. In Andong city, there are several noodle shops that serve dried noodles, but if you go to 'Park Jae-sook Farmer's House' on the way to Hahoe Village, you can taste the dried noodles. In a traditional way, the dough mixed with flour and soy flour is pushed with red sesame seeds, boiled in boiling water, dried, and then dried. Konjin noodles are perfect for summer, but they are always a favorite for those who like noodles.
The owner puts anchovy, radish, onion, and kelp into the seaweed twice a week to boil the broth with care. The pumpkin, kimchi, grated flour, and sesame seeds are gently softened, and the soft noodles are soft. It feels plain, but the soup is so deep that it is impossible to give up the last melody.

Business hours 09: 00 ~ 18: 00, closed on national holidays, Konjin noodles 7,000 won.

Gyeongsang-do chueotang, boiled with fresh local food
'Gyeongsangdo Chueotang', located in the food street of Andong City, is hidden in an alley. A traditional kitchen in a restaurant that has been renovated. Boiled hot water is boiled for 24 hours in a traditional cauldron on an old buttock. The two are running together, but the pride in Gyeongsangdo Chueotang is great. Uiseong garlic, nutritious red pepper powder, and green cabbage grown in Andong are boiled with natural loach, so it is delicious. The loach is boiled in a cauldron, crushed in a colander to filter out the bones and intestines, and then put plenty of green cabbage to preserve the sweetness.
Gyeongsangdo-style chueotang with a cool taste like clear miso soup
Chueotang is a refreshing and refreshing but richly rich protein, calcium, and mineral like a cabbage soup boiled with a little miso.
