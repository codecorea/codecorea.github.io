Children who want to play so that the ball becomes red, hold the parents' sleeves to the front door. At this time, let's plan a winter family trip to Yeongdong, Chungbuk without worry. Located at the foot of the Chubaeknyeong Mountain in the Sobaek Mountain Range, Yeongdong is famous for its high fruit sugar content as a regional feature with large daily intersections. In summer, peaches and grapes, and in autumn, apples, persimmons, and pears, if you add 'Yeongdong' to the name of the fruit, it is a fruit country. So it's easy to think that there are no things to see or eat in a frozen winter, but you are welcome. Winter in Yeongdong is full of experiences for children. World Human Rights Peace Ground, No Gun-ri Peace Park
No Gun-ri Peace Park is about a 10-minute drive from Chupungryeong Deer Tourist Farm. This is the scene of the No Gun Ri incident, which remains as a big scar in Korean modern history. It is also called the No Gun Ri massacre, and in July 1950, just after the Korean War broke out, the US military killed 250 innocent Korean refugees under the No Gun Ri railway bridge.
Nogunri Peace Park
This event, which was almost buried in history, became known to the world as a persistent truth-finding activity by survivors for over 50 years, and related laws were enacted in 2004. In addition, it created a peace park in 2011 to re-examine the sad history.
Memorial tower
The Peace Park, which covers a size of 13,240 square meters (about 40,000 pyeong), includes the Peace Memorial Hall, the Memorial Hall of Memorial, the Sculpture Park, and the Life Hall, which features the 1950s.
Memories of Life
In the Peace Memorial Museum, there are exhibits that can examine the entire picture of the No Gun Ri incident. If you look at the exhibits, you will naturally walk towards the tower of the spirits due to unhappiness and regret.
Peace Memorial Hall
The role of an adult's story bag is important in a trip with a child who is still unable to read and understand alone. Let's try an emotional sympathy to understand the pain of someone with the child, while easily explaining the concept of peace to the children.
Peace Memorial Hall
Across the road from the Peace Park, there is the Ssanggul Bridge, the site of the No Gun Ri incident.
Double-legged bridge
The No Gun-ri Ssanggul Bridge was built as a railway bridge for the Gyeongbu Line in 1934. If you look closely at the two arched bridges, the bullet marks still remain clear. The child looked at the bullet marks in the circle and seemed to feel that it was a sad scene, although not sure.

Hot wine foot bath experience, Wine Korea
The fruit that comes to mind in Yeongdong is 'grape'. Yeongdong, Chungbuk, is Korea's largest grape producing region, accounting for 12.7% of the nation's grape production. In the summer, Yeongdong is excited by the purple grape festival, but there are many wines that can enjoy the sweet grape flavor even in winter. Currently, there are over 50 winery farmhouses in Yeongdong. Let's go to Wine Korea. The cold winter wine foot bath experience melts the fatigue accumulated during the trip.
Wine Korea
Wine Korea was renovated in 1996 and was first established under the name of “Yeongdong Podo Farming Association”, and in 2004, it was renamed Wine Korea and became a joint-stock company with hundreds of farmers growing Yeongdong Podo.
Wine cellar
From the process of cultivating grapes to manufacturing, storing, and selling wine, everything related to Yeongdong grapes is managed by one stop, and the facilities in the space are diverse. From the basement wine cellar using crypts used as ammunition during the Japanese colonial period to wine bars, galleries, and private wine sellers, there are many different things to see and it is fun to see.

Full of fresh fruit scent, Fruit Nara Theme Park
From the seed to the fruit to the fruit we taste, the fruit country theme park is where you can experience the farmer's sincerity. Opened in April 2017, it is the only theme park in Korea with fruit theme. On the site of 77,950㎡ (about 23,000 pyeong), there are fruit trees such as grapes, apples, pears, peaches, and enjoy various cooking experiences including harvesting. You can. The Gugak Experience Village consists of 3 buildings with 1 floor underground and 2 floors above ground on a site of 75,000 square meters. There is a 300-seat performance hall, an experience room, and a “Gak-ak Nuri Pavilion”, a lodging space where 200 visitors can experience Korean music at once.
