Jukdo Market is regarded as the best market in Pohang. Perhaps because of the vitality of live fish, this place is always full of energy. This is why it is always exciting to see the market that there is everything except nothing. Let's go to Jukdo Market, a 'paradise of taste' and 'the scene of life' where you can taste not only various live fish but also snow crab, octopus, and Guamegi!

Jukdo-dong, where Jukdo Market is located, was an island as its name suggests. The surrounding rivers, such as Chilseong Stream and Yanghak Stream, were absorbed into the land by reinstatement. Looking at the current map, I wonder where the island was, but there were islands such as Daedo, Haedo, and Songdo in the estuary of the Hyeongsan River. Jukdo, which was a swampy area, was called 'Reed Island' because it was reeded to become 'Dae Island'. When it was changed to Chinese characters, it got the name 'Jukdo'. These islands were called 'island'. The island's large bridge guarding the Posco Bridge proves that there are now disappearing islands. An official from the Public Relations Office of the Pohang City Hall added, "Around October 2013, if the waterway connecting Dongbin Inner Port and Hyeongsan River survives after the completion of Dongbin Inner Port construction, you will be able to feel the mood of the time."

The day of Jukdo Market starts at 5 AM. It is common to think of Jukdo Market as having only fresh fish, seafood, and dried fish. The same is true if you imagined a market with a small port where you can taste Pohang Guamegi over Jukdo Market. This is not just a fish market. It is a large-scale traditional market that includes not only agricultural and food products, fruits and vegetables, but also bakery and mill food clothing shoes, hanbok and futon coma. These various items are located in 148,000 square meters (about 45,000 pyeong) of land and over 2,500 stores. It is a space suitable for the traditional market that boasts the largest on the east coast.
