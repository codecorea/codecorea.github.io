Geoje-si, Gyeongnam, Korea

Geojedo National Highway 14 is famous for its beautiful scenery. After passing Buksan Mountain and heading south to the sea, an impressive village appears. It is a gujo-la village that crosses the sea next to a land that looks as long as a giraffe's neck and thin like an ant's waist. There is a small mountain beyond the narrow and long town, so the impression that you want to look around is imminent. Entered the Gujo La Village as attracted by the strange charm.

It's past winter, but the weather seems a bit winter, and it's too cold for spring, so it's the south side of the peninsula that shines. Jeju Island is fine, but Geoje Island is a good alternative when transportation is burdensome. It leads to Geoje Bridge, so it is easy to access and has a warm atmosphere.

Beyond Geoje Bridge, you can enjoy the scenery of Namhae next to it. There are peaceful seas, islands with grains of grain, roofs gathered on the shores of fishing villages, and ships anchored in the harbor. History in Gujora Castle, Legend in Yundol Island

As you descend the ridge in front of the village, you will encounter the castle wall, the trace of Gujouraseong. This castle was built to prevent dwarfs from the Joseon Dynasty, and is one of the Geoje Chiljins constructed in the first year of Seongjong. After the Imjin War, it was moved to the side of Okpojin and then returned to its current location and was named Gujorajin and is now designated as 204 Gyeongsangnam-do Monument.

Gujora Castle Wall

When you reach the middle point between Sujeongsan and the village along the castle wall, there is one small island floating in the sea of Gujora Beach, called 'Yundol Island' and contains the legend of Hyoja's verses.
"Once upon a time, a fisherman who had a family living with a beautiful woman named Haesun lived. One day, a sea fisherman who worked at the sea disappeared by a typhoon with a ship. After this day, the fisherman came out to the sea every night and called the sea boat every night and stared at the moon. Residents who watched this figure called the fisherman 'Mangwol'.

At this time, the widow and three of his sons settled on an island called Yundol Island. The old fisherman and the widow who became widowers became soothing each other. The island where the widow lived was only able to walk to the village at low tide, and each time the widow visited the widower without her son's knowledge. Knowing these relationships, the sons of Yun made a stepping stone by placing stones between the island and the village because they felt sorry for their mother heading to the village even after taking off their socks and getting wet in the sea in winter. "
