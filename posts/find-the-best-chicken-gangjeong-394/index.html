Chicken Gangjeong, reveal the identity!
Chicken Gangjeong. A delicacy that adds sweet and spicy sauce to fried chicken. Peanuts or nuts are added to add savory flavor, and polka dots are added to emphasize the spiciness. In many ways, it is similar to seasoned chicken. But why did they name it “chicken gangjeong”? It looks similar, but once you taste it, you can tell the difference. Chicken Gangjeong, coated with malt once, has a crispy texture for a relatively long time. These days, it's not very common for the seasoning chicken to be so good that the technology has improved, but it might have been a quirky technology when it appeared like a comet after an electric roasted chicken.
'Gangjeong' is a precious food that went up to the feast and rituals along with medicine and tea ceremony. After drying the glutinous rice flour, slice it, fry it in oil, and smear it with honey and poached rice to form a Gangjeong. Once you see the process of making Gangjeong, it becomes easy to understand why 'Gangjeong' is attached to chicken Gangjeong. This is because the process of frying chicken in oil and burying the seasoning sauce mixed with honey and malt is the same as 'Gangjeong'. The sauce with starch syrup prevented it from getting wet by wearing thin clothes on the tempura, and thanks to this, people could taste chicken gangjeong that has not changed over time. That's why there are a lot of people looking for chicken gangjeong.

Let's take a look at the history of old Incheon as we climbed the opening of the port. The old place name in the Incheon area is Michu Hall. History records this town as a failed town of Biryuk Baekje and a barren land in which salty waters dig deep. However, in 1883, Incheon was reborn as a gateway to the West Sea when it was selected as the “open port” to handle the bombing of modernization. People carried things along with them. In particular, many came from Shandong, China, which was geographically close to Incheon. Just looking at the phrase ‘I can hear the chicken crying in Shandong Province’ in our old literature, we can see that the Korean Peninsula and Shandong Province were quite close. They worked to carry the luggage of the ships carrying them to Incheon Port and made “Jjajangmien” and ate it. We added “Caramel” to it to make our own Jjajangmyeon. When you go to Chinatown, the first Chinese restaurant "Gonghwachun" on earth turns into a jajangmyeon museum to greet travelers.

The home of displaced people who sounded the sound of ‘Abai’
To find this, we must go back to the Korean War. On June 25, 1950, the beautiful Mt. Geumsugang begins to turn into blood. The superficial reason is the conflict of ideology. The Korean people are divided into South and North as avatars of the great powers to fight the war. At the end of the war, during the 1/4 retreat, the residents of Hamgyeong-do, who had fled with the South Korean army, stopped here in Sokcho. You might not be far away thinking about going back to your homeland soon. The homeland, which knew that it was going to return soon, was blocked by the truce and was unable to promise a reunion. When they became displaced people, they made the food they ate in their hometown and ate them. There would have been no other way.
Abai Sundae Town located in Sokcho Tourist Fish Market
Cheongho-dong, which stayed for a while, becomes the second home of displaced people. In the village, the sound of 'Abai', which means 'grandfather' and 'uncle', became 'Abai village'. Their North Korean food made with their longing and sulwoom made this a unique tourist destination, and people flocked. There are restaurants specializing in North Korean food such as <Dancheon Restaurant>. It's a trip to cool off your head, but why is the food here not just staying ‘delicious’?
