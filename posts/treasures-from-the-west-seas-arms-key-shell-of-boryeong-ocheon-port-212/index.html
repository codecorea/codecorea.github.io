Treasures from the West Sea's arms ‘Key shell of Boryeong Ocheon Port’

Boryeong, Chungnam, Korea

There is a saying that all roads in the northern Boryeong area connect with Ocheon. This is because the port of Ocheon served as the main port and military port of Boryeong in the Baekje period. Since then, Ocheon Port has become a small fishing port, but recently it has been regaining its old glory due to the clamshell. Ocheon Port occupies about 60% of the country's production, and gourmets from all over the country gather at the port. Azalea blooming is the key season in April.

The Chinese characters for ‘오천’ are ‘Zara O (鰲)’ and ‘My Heaven’. The name is given because the terrain of Cheonsu Bay including Ocheon is like Zara. Since the mountain on both sides of the sea acts as a breakwater, there is no damage even in severe storms, and the depth of the water is a port of bliss, which is not at all uncomfortable for the passage of ships due to the tide.
It is said to be a port, but it is small. There is no change in facilities for tourists. There is a restaurant along the beach, and you can buy your favorite seafood at the restaurant. Nevertheless, the reason to visit Ocheon Port in the spring is because it is the country's largest mountain clam.
The key clam is embedded vertically in deep sandy soil at a depth of 20-50m. It is not caught by fishing, nor is it rolled up by a net. The key clam was named because the shape of the clam was similar to the key that smashed the sword of grain. It was collected from the South Sea, such as Jangheung, Jeollanam-do, and exported to Japan, but in the 1970s, it became known as a major production area for the key seashells as it became known that a lot of shellfishes lived near the Ocheon Port in the West Sea.

Galmaemot Holy Land, which holds the sad history of Catholic persecution
In the vicinity of Ocheon Port is the Galmaemot Holy Land, which holds the sad history of Catholic persecution. This is the place where five people, including Bishop Davllu, Father Ometre, and Father Wien, were arrested during the persecution on March 30, 1866. At this time, the remains of three martyred priests are placed in the basement of the Myeongdong Cathedral.
There is a reason that this distant beach became the scene of Catholic persecution. In June of the 12th year of the Joseon Dynasty (1846), three French warships anchored at the remotest island of Boryeong. Then, in the persecution of Gihae (1839), he left a letter in the box asking for responsibility for the murder of three French missionaries, including Enver, Imitation, and Father Charles. The case was regarded as an invasion of the territorial waters of Joseon by the court. At that time, the execution of Father Kim Dae-geon, who was in prison, was accelerated, and on March 30, 1866, in the sense that Heungseon-daewon troops threw a Western orangka, five French swimmers, including Bishop Davllu, took a swimming near the island of the French invasion. The bride was dragged and executed. The position of the martyrdom is now standing where the priests were executed.
