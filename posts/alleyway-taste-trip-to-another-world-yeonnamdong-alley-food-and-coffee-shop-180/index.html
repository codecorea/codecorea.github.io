Mapo-gu, Seoul, Korea

When you cross a crossing in Hongdae Street, crowded with crowds all day, you meet Yeonnam-dong, which leads to an old and alley. Narrow alleys, Yeonnam-dong Alleys, filled with analogue sensibility and vintage charm, are filled with strange and fresh taste and aroma.

There are such people. An alley that only I know, a gourmet that only goes to me. The small desire to keep everyone's own space without wanting to inform anyone seems to be selfish and timid, but the inner heart of saving that space is more affectionate and passionate than anyone else. This is the heart of people who love the alleys of Yeonnam-dong.
After walking off the bustling street at the intersection of Exit 3 of Hongik University Subway Station and walking for about 10 minutes on the left side of the street, you will see a small alley going down the side of the Kimbab House. This is a small alley leading to Yeonhui-dong, the old address of Yeonnam-dong, located at 227. Gallery, bookstore, coffee shop, and restaurant representing Yeonnam-dong are gathered in the alleyway overlooking the neighborhood at a glance.

Influencing the wind of culture and art in the alleys of Yeonnam-dong, known as Chinatown and Knights Restaurant, is a complex cultural space called 'Place Mak'

to be. It opened in 2010 and re-lighted the old alleys of the old town by collecting and displaying chairs containing the memories of residents of Yeonnam-dong. More than 30㎡, this is a non-profit space that begins as an artistic shelter for residents of Yeonnam-dong, where free and innovative exhibitions of young artists such as installation, sculpture, furniture, and paintings can be freely continued. As a small tree in front of the gallery, the gallery is like a love room in the neighborhood where anyone can look back and relax.

As you walk through the alleys of Yeonnam-dong, you'll find a bookstore that has disappeared into a faint memory due to the cafes and pubs. It is the bookstore Pinocchio (070-4025-9186), which sells picture books as the first graphic novelty store in Korea that opened a year ago.

In a 20㎡ space, looking through essays, illustration books, and handmade fairy tales full of beautiful paintings, you will be moist. There are also valuable books imported from overseas in the bookshelf, where the picture books seen by adults and children are packed together.

Tuk Tuk Noodle Thai

If you've entered the alley in search of a hot place in Yeonnam-dong, you may be disappointed at the plain atmosphere of the entrance to the alley and the simple entrance down to “Tuk-tuk Noodle Thai”. A small alley that runs smoothly is beautiful after long viewing. Tuk Tuk Noodle Thai's food is also a taste that falls out once you taste it.

Tuk Tuk Noodle Thai's representative menu, Yang Yang and Yang Tam Kai
Chefs from Issan, northeast of Thailand, say the food is exceptional. That's why Tuk Tuk Noodle Thai's food is delicious. The Thai food produced by local chefs from Issan is generous and local style compared to other places, making it especially satisfying for local food lovers. When you enter the dining room, the hammock ceiling, elephant painted walls, and exotic wooden tables create an exotic atmosphere with a unique herbal scent.
Stir-fried noodles with plenty of pork and vegetables, Pad Thai
Chicken and papaya salad with curry powder and spices added to the dish, and Yom Tam Khai Yang is a signature menu of Tuktuk Noodle Thai. The crispy fried chicken and sweet and sour sweaty salad are fresh enough to fit any dish.

Bemucho Cantina's Mexican Homestyle

Most restaurants in Yeonnam-dong are open until noon. It is a small store run by one or two people, so there are many things to prepare. “Bemucho Cantina”, with five tables, is decorated with ornaments from Mexico, the owner's hometown, and is like a pub in Mexico.

The Bemucho Cantina, shortened to the “Besame Mucho Tavern,” is a place where Mexican men and women from Yeonnam-dong meet and cook Mexican home-cooked meals. The owner, who has a strong sense of cooking experience in Mexico, offers fresh and hearty Mexican cuisine by picking local ingredients. It is basic to use fresh lettuce and tomatoes. By making use of the taste of the hometown, they produce exotic and delicious Mexican food.
Bemucho Cantina Spinach Quesadilla and Fried Chicken Tacos
Popular menu items include chicken bria, lettuce, corn, onion, paprika, sour cream, and tomato salsa sauce. Fry Chicken Taco, which is perfect for Koreans, tastes delicious with tomato salsa salad by frying the taco with chicken.
Fry Chicken Taco, the main menu of Bemucho Cantina
The husband takes care of the dishes, and the wife devotes himself to traditional Mexican drinks. Orchata, a traditional rice drink that is seasoned with sugar and cinnamon by grinding rice flour every three or four times, is often sweet and savory. There are many customers on the weekend and there are few tables, so if you make a reservation 30 minutes in advance, the table will be empty.
Cooking is more delicious with traditional Mexican beverages
Slow food, Himeji curry rice

During lunchtime, a long line of curry pours along the smell of the sweet curry. The representative menu of Himeji is curry rice. The old bicycle shop reminds me of a small home in a Japanese residential area. It is a cozy and comfortable atmosphere where the curry rice is good for indoors.

Curry rice, cooked in a Japanese style with only vegetables such as potatoes, carrots, and broccoli, is familiar to us. The popular curry udon, which is eaten with curry, is also popular. The soy sauce noodles with plenty of ginger aren't Japanese soba, but the noodles that the owner used to boil with Haejang. The soft noodle goes well with the taste of the soup that opens up inside.
Himeji reminds me of a small Japanese family home
Curry rice is topped with curry sauce that is boiled and boiled in yellow and wrinkly cooked rice with turmeric. The pieces of bell peppers sprinkled like a decoration are chewed crisp and give a pleasant taste. The scent of pepper goes very well, so I eat a lot of rice by scratching it. I have never learned cooking, no restaurant experience, and no secrets, but like the taste of a mother's hand, Himeji's curry boiled for a long time without feeling spared the essence of slow food.
