Can you hear me, the miracle of tomorrow ringing in Paju - Paju-si, Gyeonggi-do, Korea

In June 1950, the ground rang with a loud bombardment. Han River, Imjin River, Gongneung Stream, and Munsan Stream. Paju, which was called “the village on the bank,” had many roads in Oga. Today, 68 years later, Paju, arising from the pain of a dog-legged bridge, is a space that contains happiness, such as a museum and a garden cafe, touching people's wounds and dreaming of a miracle tomorrow.
